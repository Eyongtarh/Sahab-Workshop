Welding Information

MIG Welding


Carweld uses Metal Inert Gas welding, which includes two techniques: one using bare wire for joining thin metals and another with a flux core. This enables us to elding parts of vehicles, such as body panels, frames, and exhaust systems.

Stick Welding


We also do Stick welding, commonly known as arc welding by using a stick electrode welding rod. We use this in mending machinery like bulldozers, excavators, and cranes.

Tic Welding


Carweld also uses Tungsten Inert Gas welding known for its versatility. We use this for welding steel, titanium, copper, copper alloys, nickel alloys, aluminum, magnesium, Chromoly, brass, bronze, cobalt, and even precious metals like gold.

Gas Welding


We equally use gas welding and It is great for joining both nonferrous and ferrous metals. Oxygen and acetylene are the necessary kits for gas welding, very portable and no electricity is needed. We use this in fusing metals like steel, aluminum, and copper.
